You've Been Cooked

The algorithm knows what you want before you do. It feeds you content designed to keep you clicking, not content that feeds your soul. You spend more time browsing Netflix than watching anything. Your attention span has been slow-roasted by infinite scroll. You've been cooked—and you didn't even notice it happening.

We Uncook You

This is the antidote. No catalogue, no covers to scroll through, no algorithm deciding what you should read. Once a month, a package arrives at your door containing one physical book—chosen by a human, for you, based on nothing more than a conversation about what you're craving. It's slow. It's intentional. It's the opposite of everything the internet taught you to expect.

The Process

I. You subscribe and tell us what you're craving—a genre, a mood, a feeling.

II. We disappear into the book markets of Old Delhi, hunting for the right match.

III. We wrap it carefully, include a few paper extras, and send it your way.

IV. You receive. You read. You remember what it feels like.

Where We Source

Every Sunday morning, the lanes of Daryaganj transform into one of Asia's largest street book markets. Thousands of books spread across tarps, stacked in precarious towers, waiting to be discovered. We wade through the chaos—the heat, the crowds, the haggling—to find editions with character: dog-eared pages, handwritten dedications, out-of-print treasures. Each book carries a history.
